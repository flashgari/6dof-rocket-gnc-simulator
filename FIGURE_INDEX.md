# Figure Index

This page is the quick visual review path for the portfolio. Each figure below is a stable copy of a generated output artifact, with the upper-division interpretation summarized here and expanded in `docs/figure_results_interpretations.md`.

## 1. Open-Loop Disturbance Failure

Artifact:

```text
figures/week2-open-loop-failure.svg
```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week2_disturbed_uncontrolled_plots.svg
```

Key result: the disturbed, uncontrolled vehicle reaches only `3.99 m` final altitude, rotates to `177.63 deg` maximum tilt, and accumulates `25.10 m` lateral drift.

Physical interpretation: this is a coupled rotational/translational failure, not just a bad trajectory. Thrust misalignment and the CP/CM aerodynamic moment create net body torque through

```text
I omega_dot + omega x I omega = tau_dist
tau_dist = r_T x F_T + (r_CP - r_CM) x F_N
```

As the body axis departs vertical, the useful vertical thrust component collapses as `T cos(theta)`, while horizontal thrust and aerodynamic side force integrate into lateral drift. The unwrapped pitch and body-axis vertical component are included because folded tilt can falsely look like recovery after the vehicle passes through inverted flight.

## 2. PD TVC Control Comparison

Artifact:

```text
figures/week3b-control-comparison.svg
```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week3b_control_comparison_plots.svg
```

Key result: open loop fails, ideal body-torque PD limits maximum tilt to `9.88 deg`, and actuator-realistic PD TVC limits maximum tilt to `12.94 deg` with `0.0%` gimbal saturation.

Physical interpretation: the plot separates controller synthesis from actuator realization. Ideal torque proves that the feedback law can stabilize the nonlinear rigid body, while TVC proves that the requested moment can be generated by the engine-gimbal geometry:

```text
tau_TVC = r_engine x F_thrust
tau_max,TVC ~= L T sin(delta_max)
```

TVC does not behave identically to an abstract body torque because tilting thrust also changes translational acceleration. The larger TVC lateral drift relative to ideal torque is therefore a physically expected control-allocation penalty, not a plotting artifact.

## 3. LQR Control Comparison

Artifact:

```text
figures/week4a-lqr-control-comparison.svg
```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week4a_lqr_control_comparison_plots.svg
```

Key result: LQR TVC reaches `31.43 m` final altitude, limits maximum tilt to `10.30 deg`, and limits lateral drift to `10.73 m`, improving over PD TVC under the same nominal disturbance case.

Physical interpretation: the LQR is designed on the local transverse attitude model

```text
theta_dot = omega
omega_dot = tau / I
```

and then tested in the full nonlinear quaternion 6-DOF plant through the same TVC actuator model. The result is important because the linear design assumptions remain valid in closed loop: the vehicle stays near the upright operating point, gimbal saturation remains at `0.0%`, and the lower drift indicates reduced integrated horizontal thrust projection and aerodynamic side-force exposure.

## 4. Monte Carlo Robustness Summary

Artifact:

```text
figures/week4b-monte-carlo-robustness.svg
```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week4b_monte_carlo_summary.svg
```

Key result: over `100` randomized dispersions per controller, open loop succeeds in `1.0%` of trials, while PD TVC and LQR TVC both succeed in `100.0%`. Median maximum tilt drops from `177.0 deg` open loop to `12.0 deg` for PD TVC and `9.7 deg` for LQR TVC.

Physical interpretation: this is the strongest verification figure because it tests robustness against simultaneous uncertainty in wind, thrust alignment, mass, inertia, CP location, normal-force slope, and gimbal authority. Maximum tilt is a more meaningful ascent-safety metric than final attitude because a vehicle can tumble and later appear visually upright. Lateral drift is the integrated translational consequence of attitude error, horizontal thrust projection, and aerodynamic side force. LQR improving both median tilt and median drift indicates better closed-loop transverse-mode behavior within the same actuator envelope.

Artifact:

```text

```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week5_estimated_tvc_plots.svg
```

Key result: estimated-state LQR TVC reaches `31.42 m` final altitude, limits maximum tilt to `10.43 deg`, keeps maximum attitude estimation error to `0.32 deg`, and uses `0.0%` gimbal saturation.

Physical interpretation: this figure introduces the avionics layer. The controller no longer receives perfect attitude and angular rate; it receives gyro-derived angular rate and quaternion attitude estimated from noisy/bias-corrupted measurements. The accelerometer channel is interpreted as powered-flight specific force, not a clean gravity vector. The sub-degree attitude error shows that the estimator is accurate enough that feedback commands remain physically aligned with the true thrust axis.

## 8. Actuator-Limited TVC Diagnostics

Artifact:

```text
figures/week6-actuator-limited-tvc.svg
```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week6_actuator_limited_tvc_plots.svg
```

Key result: finite-bandwidth LQR TVC reaches `31.42 m` final altitude, limits maximum tilt to `11.09 deg`, and accumulates `10.67 m` maximum lateral drift. Estimated-state finite-bandwidth LQR reaches `31.43 m`, limits maximum tilt to `10.91 deg`, and accumulates `10.58 m` drift. Peak gimbal lag is about `1.50 deg`, with `0.0%` rate-limit fraction.

Physical interpretation: this figure tests whether the controller survives the actuator dynamics hidden by ideal TVC. The controller requests a nozzle angle, but the plant receives:

```text
delta_act, not delta_cmd
tau_TVC = r_engine x F(delta_act)
```

First-order lag and rate limits create phase delay between measured attitude error and applied corrective moment. The bounded tilt response and positive torque-authority margin show that the selected LQR gains remain feasible when moment is generated by a finite-bandwidth gimbal rather than an instantaneous torque source.

## 9. Variable-Mass Powered Ascent

Artifact:

```text
figures/week7-variable-mass-ascent.svg
```

Generated source:

```text
outputs/week7_variable_mass_plots.svg
```

Key result: the variable-mass actuator-limited LQR case reaches `33.32 m` final altitude, limits maximum tilt to `11.21 deg`, and accumulates `11.12 m` maximum lateral drift. Mass decreases from `50.00 kg` to `48.93 kg`, `T/m` peaks at `18.20 m/s^2`, transverse inertia decreases from `3.15` to `3.07 kg m^2`, and CM moves from `-0.080 m` to `-0.056 m`.

Physical interpretation: this figure connects propulsion, dynamics, and controls. Propellant depletion changes translational acceleration through `T(t)/m(t)`, rotational response through `I(t)`, disturbance moment arms through `r_CP - r_CM(t)`, and TVC authority through `L T(t) sin(delta_max)`. The fixed LQR controller remains stable, but the changing plant response motivates gain scheduling.
